About

In 1966, a fireball dazzled with shooting colors, smoke and speed as it burned through the atmosphere and followed the Hudson river north. Ray and Ryan Whitelaw froze as the deep space intruder showered the two with star debris. Over the course of their lives, the brothers would be consumed trying to unravel the power and meaning of the rumble-tumble of a rugby ball sized projectile, the mother meteor cast off.

After years of search Ray believes he has found the meteor's projectile and begins an astrophysical study of its nature and resulting in an Einstein - like equation, written on the pea green plywood windows of the pedestrian bridge of a commuter rail station in Yonkers N.Y on the metro-north line.

Greystone station becomes a gathering place for partying teens, a lovers lane for cheaters, and near-by neighborhood gangs. All, unwittingly drawn by the meteor projectile and Ray's growing calculus formula. On their homemade death row, "The High Five" junkies look to Ray and his magnetic overpass formula for transmutation and redemption.
